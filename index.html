Hands‑Only CPR (Adults)

Use hands‑only CPR if an adult collapses and is not breathing normally. Push hard and fast in the center of the chest at 100–120 compressions per minute. Continue until help or an AED is ready.

Steps

  1. Check responsiveness & breathing (max 10 seconds). Send someone to call 112/108 and get an AED.
  2. Position the person on their back on a firm, flat surface. Kneel beside the chest.
  3. Hand placement: heel of one hand in the center of the chest, other hand on top, fingers interlaced, elbows straight.
  4. Compress: at least 5 cm deep, allow full recoil, rate 100–120/min. Minimize interruptions.
  5. Use AED as soon as it arrives. Follow voice prompts.

Children / infants?
Children often need compressions and rescue breaths (different technique). If untrained, continue hands‑only while you call for help.

Choking (Responsive Adult)

What to do

  1. Ask “Are you choking?” If they can cough or speak, encourage coughing and watch closely.
  2. Severe choking: Stand to the side and slightly behind. Give up to 5 back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.
  3. If not cleared, give up to 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich). Alternate 5 back blows & 5 thrusts until the object is expelled or they become unresponsive.
  4. If they become unresponsive, call 112/108, lower to the ground and start CPR. Each time before breaths, look in the mouth and remove visible objects.

Never perform blind finger sweeps. For pregnant or obese adults, use chest thrusts instead of abdominal thrusts.

Severe External Bleeding

Guidelines & Precautions

  • Use gloves or a barrier if available.
  • Expose the wound quickly; do not remove objects embedded in the wound — pack around them.
  • Watch for signs of shock: pale, cool, clammy skin; confusion; rapid breathing.

Remedies / Steps

  1. Call 112/108 for life‑threatening bleeding.
  2. Direct pressure with sterile gauze or clean cloth; if blood soaks through, add more layers — do not remove.
  3. Apply a tourniquet if trained and bleeding is uncontrollable from an arm or leg.
  4. Immobilize and keep the person warm; treat for shock.

Burns & Scalds

Immediate Care

  1. Stop the burning (remove from heat; smother flames). Ensure your own safety.
  2. Cool the burn under cool/tepid running water for 20 minutes. Do NOT use ice or butter/creams.
  3. Remove tight items near the area (rings, watches), but do not peel off anything stuck to the skin.
  4. Cover loosely with sterile non‑stick dressing or clean cling film (not wrapped around a limb).

Seek Medical Help If

  • Burn is larger than the victim’s palm, deep, on face, hands, genitals, feet, or over a joint.
  • Electrical, chemical burns or inhalation injury.
  • Signs of infection: increasing pain, redness, swelling, fever.

Build a Basic First Aid Kit

Essentials

  • Adhesive bandages (assorted)
  • Sterile gauze pads & roller bandage
  • Triangular bandage / sling
  • Antiseptic wipes & hand sanitizer
  • Non‑latex gloves & CPR face shield

Medications

  • Pain reliever (paracetamol/ibuprofen)
  • Antihistamine for allergies
  • Oral rehydration salts

Tools

  • Tweezers & small scissors
  • Instant cold pack
  • Digital thermometer
  • Elastic wrap bandage
  • Tourniquet (if trained)
